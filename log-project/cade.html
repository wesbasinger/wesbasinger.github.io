On: How to identify and evaluate Logs and Exponents

Logs and Exponent evaluation

A “Log” is simply putting an exponential equation into a different form that is supposed to make it abbreviated. For example, 10^3=x would be arranged like Log10^x=2.

An exponent is a little number that is connected to another number and represents how many times that the other number is multiplied by itself.

These two mathematical expressions are actually very important to Algebra, especially Exponents. THey are a very easy and quick way to show what they show.
