Logarithms are pretty easy!

by Morgan

Posted on April 7, 2016

I have had about a week of lessons over Logarithms and after a little trouble, I understand them. I know how to move the numbers around and divide logs in a calculator. Logarithms aren’t as difficult as you would think, you just need to practice and maybe teach it to someone else like I did.

Trying to figure out for ‘x’ was more difficult though. You can experiment with numbers until you find ‘x’ or you can do it the easy way and turn it into logs. It will take some practice to learn how to write out the problem before plugging it into the calculator but it’s easy to get the hang of it.

Personally, I am not having trouble but when I have a hiccup, my friends help and explain it to me. I think I have mastered the basic logarithms and could use a little more exposure to medium level difficulty. I saw a Logarithm with the ‘3x-5’ exponent. I could probably figure it out after a while but right now it confuses me.

Now I need to try to teach the types of logarithms to others and learn how to work out the problem I stated above.

If at first you don’t succeed, try, try again

Logarithms aren’t difficult and I am glad I learned them
