About

I am a self proclaimed nerd that enjoys building indoor and outdoor furniture. The journey started with my own farmhouse kitchen table and has continued to produce all of the designs you see here.

I will build with your materials or buy them for you and factor into the final cost. I can follow an existing design or picture, along with giving my own design input. I keep my client fully involved in the process, always seeking clarification when needed.

If desired, I can produce blueprints or 3D printed models for consideration.

Rough Cedar Truss Table

This was the most recent build I've done. It went fast and I did it with some leftover wood a neighbor friend had from his deck project. The stain matches the rest of his back porch. It is a quite large table, measuring 96" x 38" on the top. I drew the plans for this from scratch and made it entirely out of 2x8 boards.

Farmhouse w/ high bench

This was the first piece I was proud of. I built this for my dining room when we moved into a new house. I just recently added the highchair bench on the end, in this picture it is not stained and I made it out of some really reclaimed wood. I like the two tone effect and it is very sturdy. As an added bonus, the chairs were a dumpster find that my wife and I refinished for "fun".

Rustic Island

My son-in-law and I made this for his kitchen in one Saturday. They decided to just use a flat stain for the finish. My only regret is the number of screw holes we had to use wood filler on. As you can see, it give them some function use of space in the middle of their expansive kitchen.

Hexagon Picnic Table

This was one of the more labor intensive builds that I did. This actually ended up at a local sno-cone shop. It is pictured unfinished here, but I did use a deck stain on it before I delivered it. It was also quite heavy and used more wood than any of my other projects.

Deck Chairs

I built this set during the height of the quarantine of 2020, when we were stuck at home for several weekends in a row. The deck stain matches our playset and took forever. The cushions were quite expensive, but it was still 1/5 the price of a comparable metal set, and I can re-stain as needed to make it last as long as I need.

Square Farmhouse Table w/ Bench

This table was commissioned by a client who wanted expand her dining capabilities for a possible expansion to her family. She wanted to use her existing chairs, so we matched the stain as best as possible with a gloss finish. This table has a matching bench and detachable legs as a feature for easy moving.
